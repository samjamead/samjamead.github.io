A framework for reflecting on lesson plans

This essay tries to incorporate everything I know about delivering a lesson where students learn. For thoughts on behaviour management in the classroom, I also wrote an essay on making reactive practice proactive.

Giving instructions

"Please ask questions now if there is anything I haven’t explained properly" is so much better than "does everyone understand". Can you dual code this?

Tell students what you want them to do, not what you don't want them to do. Imagine a student not following your instructions. Rehearse getting that student (or group of students, or class) back on task!

Show students what you want them to do, e.g. pressing buttons on a calculator for 3 part equations where you rearrange to have x 'over' y.

Student Lead

"The bulk of a lesson should be student lead," they cried. "Don't do all the work, make them do the work".

Makes sense, but how? This implies 'activities', but activities take time to prepare, so isn't this just me still doing the work, just in my evenings and on my weekends?

Plus, there's a view that activities are just timefillers, and we don't want that. What activities are actually worthwhile? What activities help students learn?
